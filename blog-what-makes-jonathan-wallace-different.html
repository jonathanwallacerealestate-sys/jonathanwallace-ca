What makes Jonathan Wallace different on Georgian Bay

Georgian Bay market · Updated June 2026 · ~4 min read

You list a Georgian Bay waterfront property. A few showings trickle in from curious browsers. The weeks pass. Nearby homes that are not as nice as yours have already sold. The difference is not the dock, the view, or the square footage. The difference is what happens behind the listing, and what Jonathan Wallace does differently to sell homes on Georgian Bay is worth understanding before you sign anything.

Jonathan Wallace is a full-time REALTOR® with Faris Team Real Estate, Brokerage, based in Midland and working across Midland, Penetanguishene, Tiny, Tay, and Wasaga Beach. The approach looks nothing like a standard MLS entry and a lockbox. It is a structured marketing engine: professional photography and drone video, YouTube home tours, data-driven pricing, and targeted buyer outreach. Each step is designed to attract serious, informed buyers rather than casual browsers.

Why Georgian Bay listings need a different selling strategy

Most listings in Ontario rely on a handful of photos, an MLS entry, and a wait. For a condo in a major city, that passive approach can still produce results. For a waterfront home or cottage on Georgian Bay, it is a costly shortcut. Buyers in this market are making an emotional and a financial decision at the same time, and they want to feel the property before they ever visit.

The buyer pool here is also unusually diverse: local upgrade buyers, families relocating from southern Ontario, retirees after a lifestyle change, and out-of-province cottage buyers, each finding listings through different channels. A single MLS entry does not reach all of them, and a listing that fails to communicate lifestyle, water access, and condition gets passed over quickly.

Professional photography and drone video: the foundation

Done correctly for a waterfront home, photography communicates scale, light, sightlines to the water, and the feel of the outdoor life. Aerial footage matters even more: one drone shot shows water access, shoreline, lot depth, and the surroundings in a single frame. For a buyer browsing from Toronto or further, that frame often decides whether they book the drive north. Industry research consistently finds that listings with professional photography draw more online views and more showings than listings without, and buyers who arrive after strong visuals arrive prepared and engaged.

YouTube home tours: buyer confidence before the showing

Jonathan Wallace publishes on-site video walkthroughs of listings on the Georgian Bay Home Tour series. These are not commercial productions designed to hide the awkward corner. They are honest guided tours: ceiling heights, dock setup, sightlines from the living room, narrated the way a knowledgeable agent talks during an in-person visit.

Buyers who watch a full tour arrive differently than buyers who saw photos. They have already walked the floor plan mentally, and when they book a showing they are close to deciding. For relocating and out-of-market buyers who cannot easily drive up for a first look, a ten-minute walkthrough replaces exploratory trips and makes the purchase feel less risky. In this market, video is not a nice-to-have. It is core strategy.

Data-driven pricing: protecting your bottom line from day one

Pricing a Georgian Bay home correctly takes more than pulling nearby sales. It takes the hyperlocal read: how each town's waterfront moves seasonally, what comparable properties actually achieved against their list price, and where supply sits right now. Jonathan Wallace reviews the full pricing analysis with sellers before the listing goes live, so you understand the logic behind the figure, not just the figure.

Overpriced listings do not just sit. They accumulate days on market, which reads as weakness to every buyer and agent browsing the history, and a mid-campaign price cut often invites low offers because buyers assume something is wrong. Getting the price right on day one keeps your listing on the right side of that curve.

Targeted outreach beyond MLS

The most motivated buyers for a Georgian Bay property are often not browsing MLS at all. They are watching YouTube, searching for specific communities, or following local content. A waterfront home in Tiny attracts a different buyer than a year-round family home in Midland; a property near Wasaga Beach draws different interest than one in Penetanguishene. Matching the marketing to the likely buyer, town by town, is how impressions become showings and showings become offers.

What sellers see when it runs together

When strong visuals, video, accurate pricing, and targeted outreach run as one strategy, the timeline from listing to accepted offer compresses. Buyers arrive pre-qualified by the content they have already consumed, and they compete against other informed buyers rather than browsing casually. Passive listings generate exposure. This approach generates demand.

Before choosing an agent for a Georgian Bay listing, ask specifically: what is your photography and video process, do you publish regular video content, how do you arrive at a list price, and how do you reach buyers outside MLS. The answers tell you quickly what kind of campaign your home would get.
