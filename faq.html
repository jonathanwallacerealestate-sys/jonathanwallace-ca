What are the best communities on southern Georgian Bay?

There is no single best, only the best fit for how you live. Midland is the hub, with the harbour, hospitals, schools and services. Penetanguishene is the historic waterfront town next door, often the more affordable entry point. Tay Township, including Victoria Harbour and Port McNicoll, offers small-community living along the Trans-Canada Trail and the water. Tiny Township is beaches and cottage country, with some of the clearest water on the Bay. Wasaga Beach is the world's longest freshwater beach with a fast-growing year-round community. Each has its own guide on the Communities page.

What should I know before buying waterfront property on Georgian Bay?

Waterfront on Georgian Bay is a different purchase from a home in town, and the differences are where deals go sideways. Shoreline type and exposure, water levels, septic and well condition, road access in winter, and shoreline regulations all affect value and daily life. Walk the property, ask what the water does in November as well as July, and work with someone who has toured and sold along this shoreline. Jonathan Wallace walks through waterfront properties regularly on his home tour series, which is a good way to calibrate what your budget buys before you ever book a showing.
